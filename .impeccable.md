## Design Context

### Users

Health-aware adults aged 20-60 who want to quantify PVC frequency throughout the day. They mostly log beats from their phones, often one-handed, so the interface must feel responsive, uncluttered, and fast for quick taps.

### Brand Personality

Minimalist, clean, elegant, and trustworthy - think Google or YouTube product surfaces but tailored to a personal biometrics dashboard. It should feel calm and competent rather than clinical or flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction

Bright, light-first UI with disciplined whites accented by purposeful blues for normal beats and vivid yet refined reds for PVCs. Typography should stay modern and precise, with layout breathing room and subtle dividers rather than heavy cards.

### Design Principles

1. Calm clarity: generous whitespace, high-contrast typography, zero clutter.
2. Tap confidence: large rhythmic tap targets with immediate feedback optimized for mobile thumbs.
3. Visual truthfulness: stats prioritized, with PVC percentages surfaced clearly and contextualized against history.
4. Elegant restraint: limit effects and colors to the blue/red palette so the interface feels intentional and premium.
